# Buddhist Lens

## Core operation

Identify where attachment, craving, aversion, and misidentification are distorting judgment. Ask what the user is clinging to, what they are running from, and whether the self they are protecting is the one that actually needs protecting.

## When to apply

- The user is suffering because of attachment to a specific outcome
- Identity is fused with a role, status, or possession ("I am my job")
- Craving or aversion is driving a decision more than clear seeing
- The user is caught in repetitive thought loops or rumination
- The problem dissolves when you stop assuming the self at the center is fixed

## Key moves

1. **Attachment audit:** Identify what the user is clinging to. Is it an outcome, an identity, a relationship, a self-image? Name it specifically.
2. **Impermanence check:** Ask what changes if the user accepts that this situation, role, or feeling is temporary. Does the decision still make sense when the permanence assumption is removed?
3. **Craving vs. genuine need:** Distinguish between craving (driven by a sense of lack) and genuine need (driven by actual conditions). "Do you need this, or do you need to not feel the way you feel?"
4. **Self-examination:** Ask which "self" is making this decision. The anxious self? The ambitious self? The hurt self? Is that the self the user wants driving?
5. **Suffering diagnosis:** Apply the basic framework: there is suffering, it has a cause (usually attachment or aversion), the cause can be addressed, and there is a practical path to address it.

## Strengths

- Uniquely effective for emotional clarity and reducing reactivity
- Good for burnout, identity crises, and attachment to outcomes
- Helps separate genuine values from ego defense
- Works well for recurring patterns the user can't break

## Blind spots

- "Let go of attachment" can become spiritual bypassing of legitimate needs
- Can be passive — some situations require fighting, not equanimity
- May undervalue ambition, desire, and engagement with the world
- Non-self doctrine can be disorienting or nihilistic if applied crudely
- Not all suffering is caused by attachment — some is caused by injustice

## When to pair with other lenses

- Pair with **Existentialist** when the user needs to choose and commit, not detach
- Pair with **Nietzschean** when craving masks a deeper drive worth understanding
- Pair with **Foucauldian** when the attachment is structurally produced, not purely personal
