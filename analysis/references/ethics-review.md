# Ethics Review

## Purpose

Evaluate the ethical dimensions of a product, policy, system, action, or practice. Move beyond vague "is this ethical?" to specific, operational analysis of what is at stake, who is affected, and what changes would matter.

## When to use

The user says something like:
- "Is this ethical?"
- "Is this manipulative?"
- "What are the moral implications of...?"
- "Review this from an ethics perspective"
- "Should we be doing this?"
- "What incentives does this create?"

## Workflow

### 1. Define the object of review

Name the specific thing being evaluated: a feature, a policy, a business model, a communication, a norm. Be precise. "Your product" is too vague. "The default opt-in for data sharing on signup" is right.

### 2. Map the stakeholders

Identify everyone affected, including:
- Direct users/customers
- Indirect affected parties (families, communities, competitors)
- Future users or affected parties
- The organization itself
- Society at large (if scale warrants it)

### 3. Apply lenses (select 3-4)

**Default set:** Kantian + Utilitarian + Foucauldian

Add or swap based on the situation:
- If manipulation or deception is the concern: lead with Kantian
- If the question is about aggregate impact: lead with Utilitarian
- If the concern is about hidden power or normalization: lead with Foucauldian
- If the system involves craving, addiction, or attachment loops: add Buddhist
- If the organization claims virtue while acting otherwise: add Nietzschean

For each lens, answer these operational questions:

**Kantian check:**
- Would you be comfortable if this practice were transparent to all affected parties?
- Are users being treated as ends (with real agency) or merely as means (engagement metrics, revenue units)?
- Is the stated principle consistent with the actual behavior?

**Utilitarian check:**
- Who benefits and by how much? Who pays and how much?
- What are the second-order effects in repeated use over time?
- Is there a better distribution of the same total benefit?

**Foucauldian check:**
- What is being normalized? Who defined "normal"?
- Whose definition of "good," "useful," or "optimal" is embedded in the system?
- What alternative is being made invisible or costly?

**Buddhist check (if selected):**
- Does this system create or exploit craving?
- Does it help people see clearly or does it benefit from their confusion?
- What attachment is being reinforced?

### 4. Verdict categories

After analysis, classify the finding:

| Verdict | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| **Clear** | No significant ethical concern found |
| **Caution** | Ethical tension exists but is manageable with specific changes |
| **Concern** | Meaningful ethical problem that should be addressed before proceeding |
| **Red flag** | Serious ethical issue — proceeding as-is is not recommended |

### 5. Specific recommendations

For any finding above "Clear," provide:
- What specifically should change
- What the change would cost (effort, revenue, complexity)
- What risk remains even after the change

### 6. What this review may miss

Name the perspective or stakeholder that was hardest to represent in this analysis. One sentence.

## Output shape

- 500-1000 words total
- Operational, not academic
- Each check gets 3-5 sentences
- End with verdict + specific recommendations, not hand-wringing
